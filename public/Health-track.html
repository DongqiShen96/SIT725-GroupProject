Other helpful information

Body Condition Scoring (BCS) is an easy-to-use quantitative tool to determine an animal's body condition and fat accumulation. The preferred BCS scale ranges from 1 (emaciated or very thin - 20% under ideal weight) to 9 (obese - 30% over ideal weight) with an ideal body score of 5.

The Association for Pet Obesity Prevention (APOP) recommends using a body condition scale that ranges from one to nine (1-9, whole integer) for dogs and cats. This allows your veterinarian to detect more subtle changes in a pet's body condition over time. Veterinarians routinely calculate and chart your dog and cat's body condition during a visit, so make sure to ask your veterinary healthcare team to evaluate your pet's body condition and teach you how to evaluate your pet for future changes.

Research shows that people often under and overestimate their pet's body condition, even when using a BCS chart. This misperception is similar to findings in the human pediatric community with children who are overweight.

Changes in a pet's weight and changes in adipose tissue can be caused by many factors: genetics, diet and nutrition, feeding habits, physical activity, age, medication, as well as an underlying disease process. It is important to know to monitor your pet for changes for subtle changes over time to ensure they live a happy, healthy, and active life.
